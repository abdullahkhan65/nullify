Based on what I know about you, I actually think your background gives you an advantage. You're not just a developer—you've worked in frontend, backend, product management, PRDs, and leading engineering teams. That means you can build quickly and understand customer needs without hiring a large team from day one.

The biggest challenge isn't being in Pakistan. It's getting your first 10–50 paying customers. Thousands of SaaS companies serving the US market are built from Pakistan, India, Eastern Europe, and Latin America. Customers generally care more about whether the product solves their problem than where the founders are located.

Here's how I'd approach it.

Phase 1: Validate Before Building Everything (0–2 months)

Don't build the AI receptionist first.

Build just enough to solve the pain:

Missed call detection
Instant SMS
Shared inbox
Booking link
Revenue dashboard

Aim to get your first paying customer as quickly as possible.

Phase 2: Pick ONE Niche

Don't target "local businesses."

Target one category.

For example:

Plumbers
HVAC
Electricians
Roofing
Dentists
Locksmiths

I'd personally start with plumbers or HVAC because:

High average job value
Frequent missed calls
Owners are often on-site and away from the phone
Clear ROI if you recover even one extra job

Once you prove it works for one niche, expanding becomes much easier.

Phase 3: Find Customers Manually

This is where many founders fail.

Don't run ads immediately.

Instead:

Go to Google Maps.

Search:

Plumbers Dallas

You'll find hundreds of businesses.

Visit each website.

If there's a phone number, they're a potential customer.

Create a spreadsheet with:

Business name
Owner (if available)
Email
Website
Phone
City

Reach out personally.

Example:

Hi Mike,

I noticed many plumbing companies lose customers simply because they're busy on jobs and can't answer every call.

I built a tool that automatically texts missed callers, keeps the conversation going, and helps recover lost bookings.

I'd love to let you try it free for 30 days and get your feedback.

Send 100–200 of these.

Don't automate initially.

Phase 4: Cold Calling

Ironically...

Call plumbers.

If they answer:

"I'm not selling advertising. I built something that helps businesses recover customers from missed phone calls."

If they don't answer...

You already know they have the problem you're solving.

Phase 5: Join Facebook Groups

Search for:

Plumbing Business Owners
HVAC Owners
Electricians USA
Contractors Network

Don't spam.

Answer questions.

Share helpful content.

Build credibility first.

Phase 6: LinkedIn

Every day, post about:

Missed-call statistics
Sales tips
Customer retention
AI for service businesses
Stories from early users
Product updates

People buy from founders they trust.

Create 30–60 second videos.

Example:

Phase 8: Referral Program

Offer:

Refer another business.

Get one month free.

Service businesses often know other business owners.

Phase 9: Local SEO

Publish articles like:

How many customers do plumbers lose from missed calls?
Best AI receptionist for electricians
HVAC missed call statistics
Roofing lead management

Over time, these can bring inbound traffic.

Phase 10: Partnerships

Partner with:

Marketing agencies serving contractors
Website developers for local businesses
Virtual receptionist services
CRM consultants

They already have your target customers.

Pricing Strategy

Avoid making it free forever.

Offer:

30-day free trial.

After that:

$79/month.

A paying customer gives much stronger validation than hundreds of free users.

Your Biggest Competitive Advantage

You mentioned wanting to serve the US and Australian markets from Pakistan.

Lean into the time-zone difference instead of seeing it as a disadvantage.

When US business owners finish work and leave feedback, you can iterate overnight. By the time they wake up, fixes and improvements are ready. That speed can become part of your culture.

What I would do if I were starting Nullify today
Build a polished MVP in 6–8 weeks.
Choose one niche (e.g., plumbers).
Personally contact 500 businesses over 30 days.
Get 10 customers, even if you onboard them manually.
Interview every customer weekly and refine the product based on real usage.
Reach $10,000 MRR before expanding into additional industries.
Add AI voice, CRM integrations, and industry-specific workflows after you've proven the core value.

One last suggestion: don't think of Nullify as a Pakistani startup. Think of it as a US-focused SaaS company with a distributed team. Register the company, pricing, website, support, documentation, and branding for your target market. If every customer touchpoint feels like it was built specifically for US and Australian service businesses, your location becomes largely invisible—they'll judge you by the value you deliver.
